Migrations
to Australia

Australia's migration and its demographics has been a crucial topic for those that reside in Australia. It is a considerable factor that influences Australia's population, economy, social, and many other important aspects of the country. Therefore, the following visualisations has been made to provide deeper understanding on migrations to Australia.

Australia's Net Migration

The waterfall chart on the right shows the changes in net migration to Australia from 2000 to 2024. The number of migrants moving in was significantly increasing from 2004 and peaked at 2008 before it began to fluctuate from 2009 to 2017. In 2018, the net migration decreased and dramatically dropped in 2020 before it rose up again to a steady figure from 2021 onwards.

Net Migration for Each State

The choropleth map illustrates the proportions of net migration of each Australian state with respect to the sum of counts of people moving in and out every month. The net migration proportions remained steady until a period between 2019 and 2020, where net migrations reached a negative figure for most states due to COVID-19 lockdowns. After that, the states' net migration proportions started to recover to its usual amount.
